02 — Colour
02

Colour
Palette

The brand palette is deliberately minimal. Black and off-white are the primary pairing. The greys create depth and hierarchy without introducing colour. This restraint is intentional — it communicates premium, athletic editorial.

Black #080808 Primary background
Off-white #F5F2EC Primary text & light bg
Surface #111110 Cards & panels
Warm grey #9A9A94 Secondary text
Rule #2A2A28 Borders & dividers
Black tint scale
100% 90% 80% 65% 45% 25% 10% Off-white
Colour usage rules
Do
Use black and off-white as the dominant pairing. Reserve grey tones for hierarchy and depth only.
Do
Use white text on dark backgrounds. Use black text on the off-white surface only.
Never
Introduce any colour outside this palette — including blue, green, red, or purple — in any brand material.

04 — Tone of Voice
04

Tone
of Voice

Meade Performance speaks like a player who's been in the trenches — direct, honest, and warm. No hype. No hollow motivation. Just the truth about what it takes, delivered with empathy and authority.

Voice pillars
01
Direct
Say what you mean. Short sentences. No padding. No filler words.
02
Honest
No hype. No overclaiming. The results speak — your job is to point at them.
03
Warm
You understand the struggle. You've been there. Empathy is the differentiator.
04
Confident
Authority without arrogance. You know what works. You don't need to prove it.
Writing — do and don't
Do write like this
"Most club players aren't unfit. They're untrained. There's a difference — and it's fixable in 8 weeks."
Short. Punchy. Speaks their language. Confident without dismissing them.
Not like this
"Our revolutionary GAA performance system will transform your athletic potential and unlock your true capabilities!"
Hyperbolic. Hollow. Sounds like every other fitness brand. Zero specificity.
Do write like this
"Follow it for two weeks. If it's not working, I'll buy it back at full price."
The guarantee said plainly. Confidence without ceremony. This is the tone.
Not like this
"We are so passionate about helping GAA athletes reach their amazing full potential with our incredible programme!"
Excessive energy. Enthusiasm without substance. Reads as inauthentic.
Voice in context
Instagram caption
"You're in the gym three times a week. Come match day, nothing feels different. That's not a fitness problem — that's a structure problem."
Problem → insight → solution implied
DM opener
"Hey Conor — how long have you been training in the gym?"
Simple. Personal. Non-threatening. Starts a conversation.
Email subject line
"You're in — here's everything you need"
Clear. Action-forward. No preamble. Delivers on the promise immediately.

05 — Photography
05

Photo
Treatment

Meade Performance imagery is editorial, not promotional. Authentic moments over posed shots. The treatment is always black and white or heavily desaturated — maintaining the monochrome brand world regardless of the original photograph.

Primary treatment
Black & white, high contrast. The default for all brand photography.
Secondary treatment
Dark overlay on lighter images. Maintains text legibility across imagery.
Texture treatment
Film grain overlay for editorial content. Instagram and long-form.
Photography principles
Authentic
Real training environments. Real athletes. Not studio lighting and fake sweat.
Purposeful
Every image should show the work — a lift, a sprint, a moment of effort or focus.
Monochrome
All photography used in brand materials must be black and white or desaturated.
Minimal
Clean backgrounds. Uncrowded compositions. The athlete is the subject — always.
